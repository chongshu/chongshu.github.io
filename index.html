Chong is a Ph.D. candidate of Finance at USC Marshall School of Business. Before entering the USC Marshall's FBE PhD program, he was a research graduate fellow at USC Institute for New Economics Thinking (INET) during the academic year of 2016-2017. He also obtained a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics/Economics and a Master of Arts in Economics at USC Dornsife.

His research interest includes financial intermediation and corporate finance (both theoretical and empirical). His current research projects involve banking competition, systemic risks, and the political economy of corporate governance. Chong had teaching experience in both economic and finance department. In addition, he had industry experience at AQR Capital Management LLC doing research on variance risk premium (VRP) in AQR's "GAP-Volatility" team.
